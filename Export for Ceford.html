AMREF UGANDA ATTENDS THE AFRICA HEALTH AGENDA INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE (AHAIC) 2023
This year’s conference shone a spotlight on the climate crisis, with sessions curated to explore themes at the nexus of health and climate change including sustainable health financing, fit-for-purpose health workforce, power of communities and regional integration. Jointly convened by Amref Health Africa, the Ministry of Health Rwanda, the African Union and Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention (Africa CDC), the biennial conference provided a platform for Africa to bring global attention to the undeniable link between climate change and health as the world inches closer to the 2030 Global Goals deadline.
 

This year, 14 staff members attended the Africa Health Agenda International Conference (AHAIC) 2023 together with representatives from one of the consortium that we lead Heroes for Gender transformative action. We also had a special invite sent out to the Ministry of Health where two delegates were supported to attend and participate in the conference.
Amref staff meeting up with Dr. Moses Mulumba one of the Panelists on the SRHR session on the right is the Heroes for Gender Transformative Action program representatives
The Conference also brought together Africa’s top thought leaders, political figures, innovators, researchers, policymakers, health workers and community mobilizers for dialogue and action aimed at mainstreaming climate discourse into health policy conversations, and vice-versa.
The Conference was opened officially with a wogging activity on 5th March to coincide with the Kigali Car Free Day, which takes place every first and third Sunday of the month as part of efforts to keep Kigali a green city and strengthen efforts to combat non-communicable diseases (NCDs). The event was covered by various media houses.
 
Dr Patrick Kagurusi, Teo Namata and Maureen Nakanja from Amref Uganda participating in the warm-up exercise in
preparation for the wogging event. Photo by Daphine Ayebare
This was followed by a series of plenaries, high-level meetings, workshops and networking sessions that took place from 6th to 8th March 2023.
Lt. Col. Dr Kyobe Henry Bbosa, incident commander for COVID-19, Ministry of Health Uganda, Dr.Richard Kabanda, Acting Commissioner Health Services in charge of Health Promotion, Education and Health Communication for MOH Uganda, Dr. Moses Mulumba, the Director-General Afya na Haki were all in attendance at AHAIC 2023 honoring an invite by Amref Health Africa in Uganda to attend and participate in the high-level debates and discussions.
 
The Objective for exhibiting during AHAIC 2023 was to showcase what Amref Health Africa in Uganda has achieved over the past year through the different projects that are being implemented by the organization and also the opportunity to engage potential partners on the journey to achieve her vision of creating a lasting health change for Africa.
 
Some of the items on exhibit by Amref Health Africa in Uganda included an overview of Amref Uganda’s work in 2022, copies of 2 of Heroes4GTA Baseline findings, knowledge products about the KOKONO crib, the KOKONO crib, knowledge products on post-COVID-19 actions, “smart technologies for Urban and Peri-Urban communities” A case study of FINISH Mondial- product copies,” among others.
 
Upwards of 500 people were able to visit the booth to view and interact with the various Amref Health Africa staff.

HEIGHTENED EFFORTS WITH INITIATIVES, WITHIN THE COMMUNITIES THAT ARE CREATING SUSTAINABLE SOLUTIONS SUPPORTED BY HEROES FOR GENDER TRANSFORMATIVE ACTION.
Meet Maria from Kalangala – Power of a Y – Hero
I was trained by the Heroes for gender transformative action program team in 2020. I was only 22 years old then and I had gone through a very difficult time of my life, I got pregnant during my senior 4 vacation. I did not have any support and I had to leave home to start looking for some casual work to support me. It was at that time that I was introduced to the Heroes program while also attending ANC. I agreed to get trained and I was recruited as a Y- hero.

I have supported over 300 youth and especially girls to access health care information especially SRH.

Since then I have supported over 300 youth and especially girls to access health care information, especially SRH. When I joined the facility as a volunteer Y-Hero champion every month we had about 15 – 50 teenage mothers, many of whom were affected due by sexual gender-based violence however to date the number of teenage mothers we get aged 10 to 15 is about 5 – 10 girls which is a big reduction.
 
At the clinic, we have been able to advocate for a day for youth health care and the facility leadership agreed that every Wednesday is youth day. This has contributed to more youth accessing services with confidence and with comfort knowing that they do not have to be judged for the services they are requesting for.
EMBRACING HEROES FOR GENDER TRANSFORMATIVE ACTION PROGRAM IN THE FIGHT AGAINST SGBV
Nabwire Sanyu and Naigaga Nubuwati reside in Buhemba village, Namayingo district. They work as focal point persons at the advisory center in Buhemba sub-county. Some of their core roles are to support in making referrals, creating awareness on SGBV, handling simple domestic violence cases and counseling. This is their story
In picture: L, Naigaga Nubuati and R, Nabwire Sanyu, focal point persons at SGBV advisory center in Buhemba sub-county
Heroes for Gender Transformative action found us in a very bad place. We were being mistreated by our partners, children were not going to school and many of the parents were not bothered. When the programme came, it supported us in creating awareness about these issues and through the different interventions we have seen positive changes. From peace in households to women supporting themselves in order to be financially independent.
 
The two ladies have since created a savings group with SGBV survivors who are able to save and borrow money according to their health needs. This initiative has enabled more women to access health care and also using the SGBV voucher system they have been able to access care and justice.
Naigaga and Nabwire have spoken to both men and women, husbands and wives and children in order to get to the root causes of disharmony in homes. This has led to a major improvement in the home environment and now the reports that they get positive from those households.
 
Naigaga testifies about the rampant Defilement cases in the community before the program was implemented however to date, they have been able to sensitize their community members through community dialogues and these cases have systematically reduced. In a week they used to get 5 to 6 defilement cases but now it is 2 to none. We applauded this milestone.

WE CONTINUE TO PROMOTE SRHR THROUGH WASH INTERVENTIONS: AMURU DISTRICT

Launching the newly constructed five-stance modern latrine with a WASH room to facilitate girls bathing and changing of their sanitary towels.

WE WALKED AGAINST MALARIA WITH MOH UGANDA AHEAD OF WORLD-MALARIA DAY 2023.
Ahead of World Malaria Day 2023, Amref Health Africa in Uganda participated in the walk against Malaria at the parliamentary grounds of Uganda on April 23rd 2023. We offered vaccination against COVID-19 with support from the saving lives and livelihoods project and shared about & advocated for the KOKONO baby crib to be included in the nationally approved Mama kit package as an intervention to protect newborn babies from Malaria.
Amref Uganda’s Communications, advocacy and fund-raising manager, Lilian Kamanzi Mugisha relaying her remarks and advocating for the KOKONO crib at the anti malaria walk
The KOKONO crib is made out of biodegradable plastic, locally manufactured in Uganda. The crib is rigid, covered with a mosquito net, washable and frugal, so as to be financially accessible to low-income communities in need of a safe space for babies during day and night time. Particularly, the crib protects newborn babies from mosquito bites and other animal bites, childhood illnesses, and accidents and can potentially aid reduction of infant mortality.

The KOKONO crib is designed particularly for the design benefit of babies 0-12 months.
 
Handing the Director of Public Health, Dr. Daniel Kyabayinze a KOKONO crib. Dr Kyabayinze pledged to continue advocating for the crib calling it a “good innovation”

AN OUNCE OF PREVENTION IS WORTH A POUND OF CURE: MARBURG VIRUS DISEASE (MVD) OUTBREAK PREPARATORY MEETING.
So far, no cases of the Marburg Virus Disease have been reported in Uganda, but measures are underway to strengthen preparedness in case of an outbreak. The need arose after cases of Marburg virus disease were reported, one in Equatorial Guinea and the other in Tanzania, with neither country reporting outbreaks before this year.

In a press conference on March 21, Tanzania's health minister announced an outbreak among a group of fishermen. Of the eight cases, five were fatal.
Meanwhile, in Equatorial Guinea, there have been 14 confirmed cases since Feb. 7, with 10 of the patients dying, according to the CDC.
Marburg virus disease (MVD) is a rare but severe hemorrhagic fever which affects both people and non-human primates. MVD is caused by the Marburg virus, a genetically unique zoonotic (or, animal-borne) RNA virus of the filovirus family.
 
The six species of Ebola virus are the only other known members of the filovirus family.
